What is a dental CT scan?

Computed Tomography or CT is a unique type of X-ray equipment that is used when regular dental or facial x-rays are not providing the desired results.
The main difference between a dental CT scan and a dental x-ray is their output. A CT scan provides a 3d image while an X-ray scan only provides us with a 2d image of the teeth. In fact, with a CT scan, dentists can look inside an entire tooth, jawbone, and even the airway without any cuts or surgery! Overall, dental CT scan is helping dentists and other professionals take precautionary measures by knowing each detail of the part of interest.

Who may be in need of a dental CT scan?

There are some indicators which should tell you that you need a dental CT scan.

  • If you have had bone grafting and plan on getting dental implants, then you might need a dental CT scan.
  • If you have had a root canal and want to know its current condition, you can have a dental CT scan for that.
  • If you are having trouble falling asleep or snore too much, you could get a CT scan.

Why is dental CT scan important?

Dental CT scan provides us with great detail of the tooth under observation. This detail can help us determine how to approach cases without causing any trouble.
If a patient needs dental implants, a CT scan can provide an exact image of how the implant should look like. If there is an infected tooth, dental CT scan can help isolate the area of infection where desist can operate without causing any extra damage..
